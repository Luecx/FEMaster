\chapter{Sections and Profiles}

Sections connect element sets to material and cross-section information. Without a matching section, an element may exist geometrically but cannot contribute the intended physical stiffness, mass, or stress recovery.

\section{Solid section}

\begin{syntax}
*SOLIDSECTION, ELSET=<elset>, MATERIAL=<material>, ORIENTATION=<orientation>
\end{syntax}

A solid section assigns a material to a volume element set. \key{ORIENTATION} is optional and is mainly relevant for anisotropic or orthotropic materials.

\section{Shell section}

\begin{syntax}
*SHELLSECTION, ELSET=<elset>, MATERIAL=<material>, THICKNESS=<t>, ORIENTATION=<orientation>
\end{syntax}

A shell section assigns material and thickness. Thickness affects membrane, bending, transverse shear, mass, and stress/resultant recovery. For anisotropic or ABD materials, orientation is part of the physical definition.

\section{Beam profiles and beam sections}

\begin{syntax}
*PROFILE, NAME=<name>
<A>, <Iy>, <Iz>, <J>, <Iyz>, <y0>, <z0>, ...
\end{syntax}

\begin{syntax}
*BEAMSECTION, ELSET=<elset>, MATERIAL=<material>, PROFILE=<profile>
<nx>, <ny>, <nz>
\end{syntax}

A profile stores beam cross-section constants. A beam section assigns profile and material to an element set. The optional direction line defines a local section direction and must not be parallel to the beam axis.

\section{Truss section}

\begin{syntax}
*TRUSSSECTION, ELSET=<elset>, MATERIAL=<material>, AREA=<A>
\end{syntax}

Truss elements use axial stiffness only. Bending, torsion, and rotational stiffness are not represented by truss elements.
