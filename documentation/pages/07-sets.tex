\chapter{Sets}

Sets are named collections of IDs. They are central to readable FEMaster decks because most higher-level commands can target sets instead of repeating individual IDs.

\section{Node sets}

\begin{syntax}
*NSET, NAME=<name>, GENERATE
<id-start>, <id-end>, <increment>
\end{syntax}

Without \key{GENERATE}, data lines are read as explicit node IDs. Node sets are used by supports, concentrated loads, couplings, connectors, point masses, and many diagnostic workflows.

\section{Element sets}

\begin{syntax}
*ELSET, NAME=<name>, GENERATE
<id-start>, <id-end>, <increment>
\end{syntax}

Element sets are used by sections, volume loads, inertial loads, RBM constraints, and topology fields. One element may belong to several sets.

\section{Surface sets}

\begin{syntax}
*SFSET, NAME=<name>, GENERATE
<id-start>, <id-end>, <increment>
\end{syntax}

Surface sets group surface IDs. They are typically used by \cmd{PLOAD}, \cmd{DLOAD}, \cmd{TIE}, and surface couplings.

\section{Naming}

Sets should be named by function, not only by geometry. Names such as \val{FIXED}, \val{LOAD_INTRODUCTION}, \val{BOLT_SURFACES}, or \val{SHELL_SKIN} remain understandable when mesh IDs change.
